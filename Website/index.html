What I do

I have dedicated significant time and effort to acquiring a solid understanding of AWS services and their practical applications.

Here are some key aspects of my knowledge and background:

Fundamentals

I have a strong grasp of the core concepts and foundational services offered by AWS. This includes understanding the basics of compute services like EC2 instances, storage services such as S3 and EBS, networking components like VPC and Route 53, and identity and access management with IAM.

Security and Compliance

I recognize the importance of security in cloud environments. I have a basic understanding of AWS security services, including IAM, AWS Identity and Access Management, and AWS CloudTrail for auditing. I'm also aware of best practices for securing AWS resources and data.

Infrastructure-as-Code (IaC)

I have been introduced to IaC practices and tools such as AWS CloudFormation or HashiCorp Terraform. I understand the benefits of managing infrastructure as code, enabling reproducibility, version control, and automation in deploying and managing AWS resources.

Monitoring and Troubleshooting

I have been introduced to AWS monitoring and troubleshooting tools, including Amazon CloudWatch for collecting and analyzing metrics, and AWS CloudTrail for tracking API activity. I understand the significance of monitoring and troubleshooting in maintaining the health and performance of AWS resources.

Cloud Infrastructure

I am familiar with the principles of cloud infrastructure design and management. I have learned about the importance of scalability, availability, and fault tolerance in designing cloud architectures. I am also acquainted with concepts like virtualization, load balancing, and auto-scaling.

Basic Networking

I have a foundational understanding of networking concepts relevant to AWS, such as IP addressing, subnets, routing, and network security groups. I am familiar with concepts like VPN connections, direct connect, and VPC peering.
